✦ THE RATING SYSTEMS ✦
Three Ways to Judge a Spell
🔮
Essence — Overall Value

The umbrella rating. How much is this EC actually worth, all things considered? Balances difficulty, time, competition, and what it gets you. Shown on every card.

🌿
Branches — Difficulty

How hard is this EC? Covers skill required, mental demand, and barriers to success. Rated 0–5 in quarter steps by students who've done it.

🦴
Bones — Time Commitment

How many hours will this actually take? Weekly time, total duration, and whether you can realistically balance it with other commitments.

🍄
Mushrooms — Competitive Entry

How hard is it to get in? Acceptance rates, prerequisites, selection difficulty. A 5 means very few applicants make it.

✦ THE HONEST GUIDE ✦
What actually makes an EC matter?
01 — DEPTH OVER BREADTH

Three ECs done seriously, documented honestly, and understood deeply will always outperform twelve ECs done at surface level. Admissions committees read activities lists carefully. They notice when everything is shallow.

02 — IMPACT IS VERIFIABLE

Raised $3,000 for a local shelter. Published two research papers. Reached 8,000 users. These are facts. "Contributed to the team's overall success" is not. Arcanum's Grimoire tracker is built to help you document specific, verifiable milestones as they happen.

03 — RARITY IS RELATIVE

A Common EC done exceptionally — leading a food bank with 200 volunteers, founding a tutoring program that reaches 50 students — is worth more than a Rare EC you participated in once. Use the Essence rating to find the ceiling, then build toward it.

04 — NARRATIVE WINS

The strongest applications have a through-line — a coherent story about who you are and what you build. Your ECs should reinforce a single narrative, not scatter across every possible category. Jebadias can help you find your thread and build around it.

05 — START EARLIER THAN YOU THINK

Junior year is when most students start thinking about ECs. The students who stand out started sophomore year or earlier — not because they were gaming the system, but because they had more time to build something real. Whatever grade you're in: now.

06 — HONEST RATINGS HELP EVERYONE

When you finish an EC, come back and rate it honestly. A 2/5 Bones rating that's accurate is more valuable than a 5/5 that isn't. The students who find this platform after you will make better decisions because of what you wrote. This is how the Arcanum works.

Codex

Arcanum's community rules and guidelines. The Marketplace is only as valuable as its listings are accurate. The Codex is how we protect that.

"I will list only what I have done. I will rate only what I have experienced. I will help other students the way I would want to be helped — with honesty, without performance."

— The Arcanum Mage's Pledge

📋 LISTING RULES
01
List only ECs you have personally participated in, led, or founded. Do not list programs you've researched but not done — there is no value in secondhand information presented as first-hand experience.
02
Rate difficulty and time commitment based on your actual experience. Overrating to appear more impressive, or underrating to attract more participants, undermines the system for everyone.
03
Descriptions must reflect what participants actually do — not what the program's website or marketing materials claim. If the reality differed from the pitch, describe the reality.
04
Include accurate, current information about costs, prerequisites, and application deadlines where known. Outdated information is better omitted than guessed at.
05
Do not list fictional, fabricated, or significantly embellished ECs. Do not exaggerate your role, the program's selectivity, or the outcomes you achieved. All listings are subject to community review.
06
Do not use the Marketplace to advertise services, products, tutoring, or anything unrelated to genuine student extracurricular activities.
🤝 COMMUNITY RULES
07
Jebadias's advice is AI-generated and reflects general patterns, not guaranteed outcomes. Verify program details, deadlines, and eligibility requirements independently before committing. Arcanum is not responsible for outcomes based on AI recommendations.
08
Reviews must reflect genuine personal experience. Do not write reviews for ECs you haven't done, do not write fake positive reviews for ECs you want to promote, and do not write negative reviews for competitive programs you want to deter others from applying to.
09
Do not use Jebadias or any Arcanum feature to generate content intended to deceive colleges, fabricate experiences, or assist with academic dishonesty. Arcanum is a planning tool, not a ghostwriting service.
10
Treat the platform as you would want others to treat it. Every listing you contribute is read by real students making real decisions. The accuracy of what you write directly affects the quality of their choices.
MODERATION & APPEALS
11
All new listings are reviewed by AI moderation before appearing in the Marketplace. The system checks for accuracy, completeness, relevance, and compliance with these rules. Listings that fail moderation are not published.
12
Any listing in the Marketplace can be flagged by any user for community review. Flagged listings are reviewed by a human within 72 hours. Listings confirmed to violate the Codex are removed or corrected.
13
If your listing was rejected or removed and you believe the decision was incorrect, submit an appeal through the Contact page with the listing details and your reasoning. Appeals are reviewed by a human and receive a response within 72 hours. The decision after appeal is final.
14
Repeated violations of the Codex may result in permanent removal of all listings submitted by that user. Arcanum reserves the right to make this determination without notice.

The Origin

What Arcanum is, why it was built, and what it's trying to fix.

THE PROBLEM

Every year, millions of students fill out the extracurricular section of the Common App by guessing. They list what they did. They describe it in 150 characters. They hope it was enough. Nobody told them what admissions readers actually look for. Nobody told them which activities move the needle and which ones don't. Nobody told them how to build a profile that makes sense as a whole. Arcanum exists because that information is available — it's just trapped behind paywalls, inside expensive counseling services, and in the heads of students who went to the right schools.

THE SOLUTION

Arcanum is a peer-reviewed EC marketplace. Every listing comes from a student who actually did the activity. Every rating reflects real effort, real time, and real results. The four dimensions — Essence, Branches, Bones, and Mushrooms — let you filter by what actually matters to you: overall value, difficulty, time commitment, and how competitive the selection process is. The advisor, Jebadias, doesn't give generic advice. He learns your situation, your goals, and your constraints, then builds a roadmap specific to you.

WHO BUILT THIS

Arcanum was built by a rising sophomore at Ballantyne Ridge High School in Charlotte, NC, who got tired of watching classmates pick ECs at random and hoping something would work. It was designed for students who want real signal, not noise — and who understand that what you do outside the classroom is as important as what happens inside it.

THE WORLD OF ARCANA

The fantasy layer isn't decoration. The world of Arcana is a metaphor for the college admissions process — a system with real stakes, incomplete information, and gatekeepers who hold knowledge the students need. Jebadias Hawthorne is an advisor who has seen every kind of student succeed and fail. He's earned the right to be direct. The Chronicle fragments are pieces of a story about what happens when a system designed to evaluate people stops actually seeing them.

WHAT ARCANUM IS NOT

Arcanum is not a guarantee. It is not a college counselor. It is not a substitute for actually doing the work, following through on what you commit to, or being honest about who you are. Admissions is not a formula. No app, advisor, or database can tell you what will work for your specific situation at your specific schools in your specific year. What Arcanum can do is help you stop guessing — and start building something real.

The Guide

Answers to every question about Arcanum, the ratings, Jebadias, and the world.

What is Arcanum?▼

Arcanum is a peer-reviewed extracurricular marketplace for high school students. Every EC in The Stacks was added and rated by students who actually did it. The four rating dimensions let you filter by what matters — overall value, difficulty, time commitment, and how competitive the application process is — instead of guessing from a blank list.

What do the four ratings mean?▼

🔮 Essence — Overall value. Does this activity move the needle with admissions?

🌿 Branches — Difficulty. How much skill, knowledge, or preparation does it genuinely require?

🦴 Bones — Time commitment. How many hours per week does it realistically demand?

🍄 Mushrooms — Competitive entry. How hard is it to get in, place, or be selected? A 5 means under 5% acceptance.

Who is Jebadias?▼

Jebadias Hawthorne is the Academy's advisor. He is not a general-purpose AI. He asks targeted questions about your grade, goals, interests, time, and constraints — then builds a personalized EC roadmap for your situation. He has nine personas spanning his 237-year history, from his first months as an advisor at age 23 to the Oracle he becomes centuries later. They unlock as you explore.

What is the difference between Spellbook and Grimoire?▼

Spellbook is your wishlist. Save any EC you're considering without committing. Browse freely. Add generously. Your Spellbook persists across sessions automatically.

Grimoire is your active tracker. When you commit to pursuing an EC, cast it from your Spellbook into your Grimoire. Log milestones, track status, and record outcomes. By application time, your Grimoire will contain everything you need to write your activities section.

How do I add an EC to the database?▼

Go to The Quill. Fill out the form honestly — name, category, description, what you actually did, and all four ratings. The Codex covers what makes a listing valuable versus one that misleads other students. Submissions save to your device. The Academy reviews all submissions periodically.

Does Arcanum store my data anywhere?▼

Your Spellbook, Grimoire, EC submissions, Chronicle fragments, and settings are all saved locally in your browser using localStorage. Nothing is sent to any server — everything stays on your device. Clearing browser data will reset your saved information. Cloud sync is planned for a future phase.

What are Chronicle fragments?▼

Chronicle fragments are pieces of hidden lore scattered across the World of Arcana. Click locations on the map to discover them. Some are found immediately. Some require clicking the same location multiple times. Some require finding things that are not labeled. The Chronicle page shows your collection and the story they form when read together.

Is Arcanum free?▼

Yes. Arcanum is completely free. There are no ads, no premium tiers, and no paywalled features. The database, the advisor, the tracker, and the map are all available without any account or payment. That is not changing.

Why is there a fantasy world layer?▼

The world of Arcana is a metaphor for the college admissions system. The Academy is the institution that holds the knowledge students need. Jebadias is an advisor who has watched thousands of students succeed and fail over two centuries. The Order of the Empty Throne — which believed extracurricular achievements should be determined by birth — is what happens when access is restricted by privilege rather than effort. The Chronicle tells the rest.

How do the filters in The Stacks work?▼

Filters are multi-select. Click any category to filter by it. Click multiple categories to see ECs across all of them. The impact and rarity rows work the same way. Click a selected filter again to deselect it. "All" resets everything. The sort dropdown sorts the filtered results by whichever dimension matters most to you right now.
